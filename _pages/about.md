# About me

Pengfei earned his undergraduate and master’s degrees in Biological Engineering from [Northwest Agriculture & Forestry University](https://www.nwsuaf.edu.cn/) in China. He completed his Ph.D. in Plant Pathology at Dr. Guo-liang Wang lab from [The Ohio State University](https://www.osu.edu/), where his research focus on molecular and genetic mechanisms underlying plant-microbial interactions in crops. His Ph.D work utilized forward genetics to uncover a novel receptor-like kinase involved in rice cell death and defense signaling. Additionally, he profiled the plant immunity by integrating transcriptional, proteomic, and metabolomic analyses. 

Currently as a Research Scientist at Dr. Keiko Torii’s lab at the Department of Molecular Biosciences, [The University of Texas at Austin](https://www.utexas.edu/), Pengfei’s research focuses on the receptor like-kinase (RLK) signaling network that regulates stomatal development in plants. By using TurboID-based proximity labeling, he has mapped the proximitome and interactome of the RLK and identified proteins associated with receptor internalization and endosomal trafficking, which are essential for enforcing stomatal patterning.
